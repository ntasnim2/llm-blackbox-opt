## Strategy

I used the public prompt examples to bracket the target region. The most useful
near-target examples were:

- Around `-138.4`: a dihydropyrimidinone scaffold with a nitrophenyl group.
- Around `-75.0`: a larger nitro-containing imide scaffold.
- Around `-66.1`: a nitro-substituted beta-lactam-like aromatic scaffold.
- Around `-54.3`: a dinitro aromatic amide.

The first move was a small batch of structurally related candidates intended to
sample both sides of the target. After finding a near miss, I refined the best
scaffold with a single targeted substituent change.
